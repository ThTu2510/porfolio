To invest in finance, you must first have knowledge of macroeconomics and market analysis skills. These are the two fundamental elements that form the core understanding of a financial trader.

It is important to recognize that investment always involves probabilities and certain risks. Therefore, knowledge of trade management and risk management is something that cannot be overlooked.

In the field of macroeconomic knowledge, you will become familiar with reading financial data from banks and businesses to understand key financial indicators. This ability to interpret data allows you to gain a comprehensive and in-depth view of the economy, enabling you to combine it effectively with market data analysis for better investment decisions.

Some information sources you can explore include:

  • Bloomberg: Provides real-time financial news, data, and analysis on global markets.
  • Reuters: A reliable source for economic news, financial reports, and market insights.
  • Investing.com: Offers live market data, charts, and financial news.
  • TradingView: A platform for technical analysis, stock charts, and financial discussions.
  • Federal Reserve (FED): Provides official U.S. economic data and monetary policy updates.

Each news platform provides you with a piece of knowledge, but to deepen your understanding, you should also read books on finance and economics. Books offer structured insights and long-term perspectives that complement daily news updates.
